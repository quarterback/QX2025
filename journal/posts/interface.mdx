Interfaces are not neutral. They curate access, normalize behavior, and scaffold expectations—often invisibly.

What we think of as “ease of use” is sometimes just “ease of control.”

---

> “To design a system is to design the default consequences of its use.” – Unknown

---

Whether we’re tapping through a help flow or attempting to delete our account, the interface teaches us the limits of our agency. And what it conceals is often more important than what it shows.

<YouTubeEmbed url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cbYr75_R15M" rounded />

That’s why **Consequence Design** begins not with intention, but with effect. It interrogates how interfaces and technology proliferate daily life—and how those implementations shape our collective options, capacities, and futures.
